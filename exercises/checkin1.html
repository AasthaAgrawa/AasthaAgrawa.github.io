Design Thinking Process

Design thinking is a non-linear, iterative process that teams use to understand users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems and create innovative solutions to prototype and test (Interaction Design Foundation - IxDF. (2016, May 25)).

What is the Design Thinking Process?

The five process:

  1. Empathize
  2. Define
  3. Ideate
  4. Prototype
  5. Test

Why does it matter?

The first rule of this process is to design for the user. A lot of the times, people blame themseleves for not being able to use a device or an application correctly. When in fact, it's the design's fault. Design thinking process helps the designers to think of these challenges that might be faced by their target users and avoid or solve such problems. By using this process, one can even navigate accessibility issues, making it inclusive for everyone!

The Design of Everyday Things

The first edition of the book (1988)

Don Norman, in his book "The Design of Everyday Things", describes the designs issues that we come across daily.
Some topics of the book include:

  • The Psychopathology of Everyday Things
  • The Psychology of Everyday Actions
  • Knowledge in the Head and in the World
  • Knowing What to Do
  • To Err Is Human
  • Human-Centered Design
  • The Design Challenge

He went to great lengths to define and explain these terms in detail, giving examples following and going against the advice given and pointing out the consequences (Wikipedia).
